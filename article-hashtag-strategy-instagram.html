Hashtag Strategy for Instagram and Twitter in 2026 — Free Generator

June 2026 5 min read

Hashtags done right can put your posts in front of thousands of people who have never heard of you. Done wrong, they look like spam and get your account suppressed. Here is what actually works in 2026.

How many hashtags to use in 2026

Instagram: 3–5 highly relevant hashtags outperform 30 generic ones. Instagram's own data shows posts with 3–5 targeted hashtags get better reach than those with 20+. The algorithm now prioritises content relevance over hashtag volume.

Twitter/X: 1–2 maximum. More than 2 hashtags on Twitter reduces engagement by up to 17% according to published studies.

LinkedIn: 3–5 professional, topic-specific hashtags. Avoid generic tags like #business or #success — too broad to drive quality engagement.

Three types of hashtags to mix

  • Niche hashtags (5,000–100,000 posts): Best for reaching engaged audiences. #FreelanceDesigner, #UIUXDesign, #BrandIdentityDesign
  • Medium hashtags (100,000–500,000 posts): Moderate competition, good visibility. #GraphicDesign, #WebDesign, #LogoDesign
  • Broad hashtags (1M+ posts): High competition, limited value. Use sparingly as one of your 3–5

How to research hashtags

  1. Search your main keyword on Instagram and check the "Related" section
  2. Look at what hashtags your top competitors use on their best-performing posts
  3. Check hashtag size — aim for niche and medium tags, not oversaturated ones
  4. Rotate hashtag sets to avoid being flagged as repetitive

Hashtags for designers

High-performing design hashtags: #UIDesign #UXDesign #BrandDesign #FreelanceDesigner #DesignerLife #GraphicDesigner #LogoDesigner #DigitalMarketing #CreativeDirection #DesignInspiration

Do hashtags still work in 2026?

Yes, but the strategy has shifted. Fewer, more targeted hashtags now outperform large hashtag lists. Instagram's algorithm now uses semantic understanding of post content — hashtags supplement this but no longer drive it.
